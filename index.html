The Death of Our Beaches

Climate change is affecting more than just the polar bears, it affects our daily live. The trend of global warming has created a looming threat upon our beaches that has been increasing over past decades, due to preventable human activities.

  • Get Started

The Cause

An average Americans pumps out 17 tonns of Carbon Dioxide per year. Multiply this number by millions and it is clear to see the devastation humans can cause. This carbon, methane, and other emmissions society brings about does not just disappear. Instead it ends up everywhere even our most beloved places, such as beaches.

Rising Tides

Over 97 % of scientists agree that the climate is changing, why can't we? The beaches are dying, the rising tides eliminate potential safe areas, while increasing frequency of storms brings about a much more dangerous ocean. When there is so much on the line why can't society come to a consensus.

What could you do?

Now that you know the risks, see what you could do in order to Bend the Curve?

Go Solar

Solar could help reduce the coal resources used in creating energy and cause the climate to keep in check

Eat less meat

Try to diversify your diet in order to decrease the ammount of livestock contributing to the heating.

Public transport

Using a bus or other methods to get to the beach, will help contribute to well being of the beach.

Eco Friendly Appliances

Putting to use clean and renuable products in your home will assist in keeping emmissions low and sea tides lower.

Use the Tap

All the plastic trash ends up in the beaches ruining the ecosystem and feel of the beach. Using tap water will reduce the usage of this plastic.

Start Voting

Vote in local elections for changes that are direct to your lifestyle, such as beaches.

Spread The Knowledge

Letting others know about this issue also causes public officals to take a serious tone with matters concerning you!
